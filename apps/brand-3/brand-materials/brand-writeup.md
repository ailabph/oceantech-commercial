## 3. About Section Copy

**Section Headline:**
This isn't a company story. It's a diving story.

**Paragraph 1:**
It started in 1981 in Maribago — a small stretch of coast on Lapu-Lapu City's eastern shore, where the Cebu Strait runs deep and the current teaches you respect fast. Back then, it was recreational diving. Reef work. Taking people down and bringing them back up. The founders were the first PADI-certified instructors in the Philippines — pioneers who didn't just learn to dive, they helped set the standard for an entire country. You learn a lot in water like that. You learn how to read what the ocean is telling you. You learn that the plan you made on the surface doesn't always survive the first ten meters. And you learn that the only gear that matters is the stuff that works when things go sideways. This has always been a family operation — the knowledge, the discipline, the deep respect for the water passed down through generations.

**Paragraph 2:**
That went on for 36 years. Thirty-six years of salt water, sun, and solving problems at depth before Oceantech Commercial Diving became an actual company in 2016. But the truth is, by then, the work had already been the work for a long time. The transition to commercial diving — welding, cutting, hull maintenance, structural repair, NDT inspection — wasn't a reinvention. It was just putting a name to what the crew had been building toward for decades.

**Paragraph 3:**
We're still based in Maribago. The boats still go out from the same water. The difference now is the jobs are bigger, the stakes are higher, and the people calling us need things done right the first time. That part hasn't changed, actually. It was always like that.

**Pull-Quote:**
_"We didn't start this company because we saw a market opportunity. We started it because we'd already been doing the work for 36 years and figured it was time to make it official."_

## 4. Services Section Copy

**Section Headline:**
Here's what we actually do.

**Intro:**
No fancy categories. No marketing names for simple things. This is the work — the stuff our divers gear up for, get wet for, and get done. If you've got a job that needs doing underwater, chances are it's on this list.

---

### Underwater Welding
Steel doesn't care that it's submerged — it still needs to hold. Our divers run wet welds and habitat welds on hulls, pipelines, and structural steel. The welds are coded, the divers are certified, and the work holds up because it has to.

### Hull Cleaning
Marine growth is a tax on every vessel that sits in the water. It slows you down, burns more fuel, and eats your coatings alive. We scrape it, blast it, and get your hull back to where it should be — without pulling you into dry dock.

### Structural Repair
Piers crack. Pilings corrode. Things underwater break in ways nobody planned for. We go down, assess the damage, and fix it. Concrete, steel, whatever the structure — we've patched, reinforced, and rebuilt enough of it to know what a lasting repair actually looks like.

### Pier & Wharf Cleanup
Port infrastructure collects debris, growth, and damage the way a dock collects barnacles — constantly, and worse than you think. We clear it out, clean it up, and tell you what we found down there. Sometimes that report is the most valuable part of the job.

### Ship & Vessel Maintenance
Propeller polishing, anode swaps, valve work, sea chest clearing — the kind of in-water maintenance that keeps a vessel running between dry dock visits. We handle it so your schedule doesn't have to bend around a haul-out you weren't planning for.

### Survey & NDT Inspection
Sometimes you don't need a repair — you need to know what's going on down there before you decide anything. We run ultrasonic thickness checks, visual and CCTV surveys, and put together reports that give you the real picture. No sugarcoating. Just measurements and honest assessments.

## 5. Process Section Copy

**Section Headline:**
How a job works with us.

### Step 1 — You tell us what's going on.
Call, email, whatever works. Tell us what the problem is, what the vessel or structure looks like, and what you need done. We'll ask questions. Some of them might seem obvious, but the obvious stuff is usually what saves time later.

### Step 2 — We go look at it.
Before anyone starts cutting or welding, we assess the site. Water conditions, access, depth, current, what the structure actually looks like versus what the drawings say. We build a dive plan from what we find, not from what we hope to find.

### Step 3 — We do the work.
Crew shows up with the right equipment and the right people. The dive goes according to the plan. There's constant communication between the diver and the surface team. Safety procedures aren't optional — they're the reason everyone goes home.

### Step 4 — You get the full picture.
When it's done, you get a report. Photos, video, measurements, what we found, what we did, and what we'd recommend keeping an eye on. It's thorough because you're going to need it — for your records, for class, for the next time someone asks what's happening below the waterline.

## 6. Why Us Section Copy

**Section Headline:**
Why people keep calling us back.

### 1. We've been in this water a long time.
Since 1981. That's not a number we throw around to sound impressive — it's just how long we've been doing this. The ocean around Cebu has been our training ground, our classroom, and our workplace for over four decades. That kind of time in the water teaches you things no certification course will. Our founders were the first PADI-certified instructors in the Philippines — they helped build the diving community in this country from the ground up. This is a family business, and that means the experience doesn't just accumulate — it gets passed down.

### 2. Safety is the job, not a sidebar.
Every dive has a plan. Every plan has a safety protocol. Every crew member knows the protocol isn't a suggestion. We take this seriously because the alternative — not taking it seriously — isn't something we're willing to live with. Everyone goes home. Every time.

### 3. We own our equipment.
We're not renting gear for your job. Our diving systems, welding rigs, cutting equipment, and inspection tools are ours — maintained, checked, and ready. When you call, we don't need to make calls. We mobilize.

### 4. We say what we find.
Our reports don't soften bad news. If the structure is deteriorating, we'll tell you it's deteriorating. If the repair will hold another five years, we'll say that too. You're paying us for the truth underwater, not for the version that's easiest to hear.

### 5. Cebu is home. We get there fast.
Operating out of Maribago puts us right on the water with quick access across the Visayas. We mobilize fast because we're already close, and we know the local waters, tides, and conditions without having to look them up.

### 6. The price is the price.
We quote the job based on what it actually takes. No hidden mobilization fees showing up after the fact. No mysterious line items. You'll know what you're paying for before we load the boat.
