## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Story

Oceantech Commercial Diving was not built on good intentions. It was forged from four decades of operational depth.

Our founders entered the water in 1981 as recreational divers off the coast of Cebu — becoming the first PADI-certified diving instructors in the Philippines. Over the years that followed, they accumulated something that cannot be purchased, fast-tracked, or fabricated: an intimate, hard-earned understanding of what it means to work beneath the surface. They learned to read structures by feel, to operate in zero visibility, to trust their training when the environment offered nothing else.

When Oceantech was established as a commercial diving operation in 2016, the directive was singular: build a firm that treats every underwater operation as an act of engineering. No approximation. No improvisation. Every weld, every cut, every inspection executed to specification.

Based in Maribago, Lapu-Lapu City, Cebu, in the industrial heart of the Central Visayas, we serve ship owners, port authorities, marine contractors, and energy operators who demand precision at depth. We do not compete on price. We compete on the quality of the work product and the discipline of the crew that delivers it.

### Mission

To execute commercial diving operations at an engineering-grade standard of precision — delivering underwater welding, cutting, inspection, and marine maintenance services backed by over four decades of accumulated mastery and an absolute refusal to compromise.

### Vision

To be the definitive standard for commercial diving in the Philippines — the operation that ship owners, port authorities, and international marine operators deploy when the margin for error is zero.

### Core Values

1. **Precision Above All** — Every cut, every weld, every measurement is executed to specification. We do not approximate. We do not estimate where exactness is required. Precision is the foundation of everything we deliver.

2. **Mastery Through Discipline** — Our team carries over four decades of accumulated knowledge in the water, rooted in a family legacy that began when our founders became the first PADI-certified instructors in the Philippines. That experience is not nostalgia — it is operational advantage, sharpened by continuous training and relentless self-improvement.

3. **Uncompromising Accountability** — We own every outcome. If we committed to a specification, we meet it. If we committed to a timeline, we hold it. No excuses. No qualifications. Our word is our contract before the paperwork is signed.

4. **Strength Under Pressure** — Commercial diving operates in conditions that punish weakness — limited visibility, extreme environments, unforgiving physics. We are built for pressure. We perform when it matters most.

5. **Relentless Excellence** — Good enough is not a standard we recognize. Every operation is an opportunity to demonstrate that the best commercial diving in the Visayas is not a matter of opinion — it is a matter of record.

### Positioning Statement

For ship owners, port authorities, and international marine operators who demand the highest standard of underwater work in the Visayas and greater Philippines, **Oceantech Commercial Diving** is the premium commercial diving operation that delivers **engineering-grade precision backed by over four decades of accumulated mastery**, because we have built our operation around a single principle — that commercial diving is a discipline of exactness, and anything less than flawless execution is unacceptable.

### Brand Personality

**Archetype:** Creator-Ruler Hybrid

- **Creator:** Master craftsman, obsessive attention to detail, pride in the quality of the work itself. Every weld is a signature.
- **Ruler:** Commanding authority, sets the standard others follow, refuses to compromise. We do not compete. We set the benchmark.

**Personality Traits:**

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Bold | Unapologetic confidence in our capabilities and standards |
| Precise | Obsessive attention to exactness in every operation |
| Commanding | Authority earned through demonstrated excellence, not volume |
| Unwavering | Immovable standards, consistent delivery, no exceptions |
| Elite | Operating at a level that separates us from the field entirely |

### Taglines

1. **Depth demands discipline.** (Primary)
2. Below the waterline. Above the standard.
3. Built for the work others won't touch.
4. Precision at depth. Results on the surface.
5. We don't surface until it's done.

**Heritage callout line:**

## 2. Logo

### 2.1 Logo System Overview

The Oceantech "Industrial Precision" logo system consists of four primary components:

| Component | Description | Primary Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Icon Mark** | Angular geometric mark — sharp, engineered, precision-cut | Favicons, avatars, app icons, equipment marking, hard hats |
| **Wordmark** | "OCEANTECH" in heavy condensed all-caps (Bebas Neue) | Headers, signage, wide-format applications |
| **Combination Mark (Horizontal)** | Icon + wordmark side by side | Primary use on website, documents, wide spaces |
| **Combination Mark (Stacked)** | Icon above wordmark with optional tagline | Square formats, social media, vertical spaces |

**Logo Concepts Developed:**

- Angular geometric "O" icon with sharp diagonal cuts (depth and precision motif)
- Industrial "OT" monogram with angular intersecting geometry
- Chevron/diamond depth motif (downward movement and structural integrity)
- Precision mark with angular intersecting lines
- Heavy combination lockups with condensed industrial wordmark
- Stamped/stencil-quality industrial marks

### 2.2 Approved Color Variations

The logo is designed for dark-background-first usage. The reversed (light) version is the standard, default treatment.

| Variation | Usage | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Reversed — Cool White** | Default use on Deep Navy / dark backgrounds | Primary treatment. This is the standard. |
| **Reversed — Gold accent** | Premium applications on dark backgrounds | Icon or separator line in Electric Gold, wordmark in Cool White |
| **Full Color — Dark** | Light background applications | Deep Navy icon + Deep Navy or True Black wordmark |
| **Single Color — Deep Navy** | Monochrome formal documents | Entire mark in #0B1426 |
| **Single Color — Black** | Newsprint, low-fidelity reproduction | Entire mark in #000000 |
| **Single Color — White** | Use on photographic or colored backgrounds | Entire mark in #FFFFFF |

Never render the logo in unapproved colors, gradients, or effects. The logo must never appear with drop shadows, bevels, glows, outer strokes, or any dimensional treatment.

### 2.3 Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo on all sides equal to the height of the icon mark. This zone must remain free of text, imagery, borders, or other graphic elements.

```
    ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
    │            (clear)               │
    │                                  │
    │    ┌────────────────────┐        │
    │    │                    │        │
    │    │    [LOGO MARK]     │        │
    │    │                    │        │
    │    └────────────────────┘        │
    │                                  │
    │            (clear)               │
    └──────────────────────────────────┘

    ◄──►  Clear space = height of icon mark
```

For the wordmark-only configuration, the clear space is equal to the cap height of the letter "O" in "OCEANTECH."

### 2.4 Minimum Size

| Format | Minimum Height | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Digital — Combination Mark** | 40px | Full mark with legible wordmark |
| **Digital — Icon Only** | 24px | Favicon and small UI contexts |
| **Print — Combination Mark** | 15mm | Business cards, small print |
| **Print — Icon Only** | 10mm | Embroidery, equipment marking, small merchandise |

Below these sizes, switch to the icon-only mark.

### 2.5 Incorrect Usage

The following treatments are never permitted:

- **Do not** stretch, skew, or distort the logo in any direction.
- **Do not** rotate the logo.
- **Do not** apply drop shadows, bevels, glows, or 3D effects.
- **Do not** apply gradients of any kind to the logo.
- **Do not** place the full-color logo on a busy photographic background without a solid or semi-transparent dark backing.
- **Do not** change the relative proportions between the icon and wordmark.
- **Do not** rearrange logo components.
- **Do not** add outlines or strokes to the logo.
- **Do not** use the logo at low resolution where details become illegible.
- **Do not** place the logo in a container or decorative frame unless it is an approved lockup.
- **Do not** use unapproved color combinations.
- **Do not** place the dark-version logo on a dark background or the light-version logo on a light background.

### 2.6 Co-Branding

When the Oceantech logo appears alongside a partner or client logo:

- Maintain equal or greater visual prominence for the Oceantech mark.
- Separate logos with a vertical divider line (1px, Electric Gold #D4A017) or adequate clear space.
- Never merge, overlap, or interlock the Oceantech logo with another brand's mark.
- On dark backgrounds, use a gold divider. On light backgrounds, use a navy divider.

### 3.4 Color Application Principles

- **Dark-dominant layouts.** Deep Navy and True Black form the foundation. This brand lives in dark mode. Dark backgrounds are the default, not the alternate.
- **Gold as signal.** Electric Gold is used sparingly and with intention — for key data points, precision marks, critical labels, CTAs, and moments of emphasis. It is not decorative. It is functional. Overuse diminishes its power.
- **Steel Silver for structure.** Secondary text, dividers, technical annotations, and UI chrome. It carries information without competing with gold.
- **Cool White for contrast.** Primary text on dark backgrounds, clean negative space, sharp typographic edges.
- **True Black for depth.** Deeper than navy — used for sections that need maximum weight or to create layered depth in dark layouts.

### 3.5 Color Usage Ratios

| Proportion | Color | Application |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| **60%** | Deep Navy + True Black | Backgrounds, structural foundation, dark surfaces |
| **20%** | Cool White | Primary text, headings on dark, breathing room, negative space |
| **12%** | Steel Silver | Secondary text, technical elements, dividers, annotations |
| **8%** | Electric Gold | CTAs, highlights, accent marks, premium details |

### 3.6 Accessible Color Combinations

The following combinations meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast requirements:

| Foreground | Background | Contrast Ratio | Use Case |
|------------|------------|----------------|----------|
| `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | 15.8:1 | Primary text on dark backgrounds |
| `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | `#111111` (True Black) | 17.4:1 | Text on maximum-dark backgrounds |
| `#D4A017` (Electric Gold) | `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | 7.1:1 | Gold accents, CTAs on dark backgrounds |
| `#D4A017` (Electric Gold) | `#111111` (True Black) | 7.8:1 | Gold accents on black backgrounds |
| `#8C9EAF` (Steel Silver) | `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | 5.2:1 | Secondary text on dark backgrounds |
| `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | 15.8:1 | Dark text on light backgrounds |
| `#111111` (True Black) | `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | 17.4:1 | Body text on light backgrounds |

**Caution:** Steel Silver (#8C9EAF) on Cool White (#F0F2F5) does not meet AA contrast for body text. Use it only for large decorative text (24px+) or non-essential visual elements on light backgrounds. For readable secondary text on light backgrounds, use Deep Navy at reduced opacity or a darker steel tone.

### 3.7 Color Combinations to Avoid

- Electric Gold text at small sizes on Cool White backgrounds (insufficient contrast for body text).
- Steel Silver text on Cool White backgrounds at any size below 24px.
- Any brand color on a photographic background without a solid or semi-transparent dark overlay.
- Cool White text on Electric Gold backgrounds (poor contrast and off-brand).
- Full-saturation gold as a background fill (gold is an accent, never a surface).

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Typeface System

The Oceantech "Industrial Precision" type system uses five typefaces, each serving a distinct and non-negotiable role:

#### Bebas Neue — Primary Heading Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Heavy condensed sans-serif |
| **Weights Used** | Bold (700) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Industrial, commanding, high-impact |
| **Usage** | H1 and H2 headings, hero headlines, section titles, large display text |

Bebas Neue is the typographic hammer of this brand. All caps. Tight tracking. Maximum vertical impact in minimum horizontal space. It commands attention and does not negotiate for it. Used exclusively for primary and secondary headings.

#### Oswald — Secondary Heading Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Condensed sans-serif |
| **Weights Used** | SemiBold (600), Medium (500) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Structured, authoritative, technical |
| **Usage** | H3 and H4 headings, subheadings, navigation labels, button text |

Oswald bridges the gap between Bebas Neue's raw impact and Inter's quiet readability. It carries authority at smaller sizes where Bebas Neue would lose legibility, and provides a secondary tier of hierarchy without breaking the condensed, industrial character.

#### Inter — Body Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Humanist sans-serif |
| **Weights Used** | Regular (400), Medium (500) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Clean, technical, highly readable |
| **Usage** | Body text, paragraphs, descriptions, form fields, list items, captions, general UI text |

Inter is the operational workhorse. Optimized for screen readability across both dark and light contexts, it keeps long-form content comfortable and accessible. Its clean geometry reinforces the engineering identity without competing with the display typefaces.

#### JetBrains Mono — Technical / Monospace Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Monospace |
| **Weights Used** | Regular (400) |
| **Source** | JetBrains — free, open source |
| **Character** | Technical, precise, data-oriented |
| **Usage** | Technical specifications, measurement data, certification numbers, coordinates, equipment codes |

JetBrains Mono appears wherever data demands precision — depth readings, weld codes, certification numbers, equipment specifications. It signals that the information it carries is exact, verified, and engineered.

#### Libre Baskerville — Heritage Accent Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Transitional serif |
| **Weights Used** | Italic (400i) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Established, editorial, refined |
| **Usage** | "Est. 1981" heritage callouts only, occasional editorial moments, pull quotes |

Libre Baskerville is used with extreme restraint. It exists solely to mark the brand's heritage — the italic weight appears for "Est. 1981" callouts and, rarely, for editorial pull quotes that require a moment of gravitas. It must never replace Oswald or Bebas Neue for structural headings. Maximum usage: 1-2 instances per page.

### 4.4 Typography Rules

1. **Dark mode default:** All heading and body colors default to light-on-dark. Light mode is the override, not the base.
2. **Heading hierarchy is strict:** H1 and H2 use Bebas Neue only. H3 and H4 use Oswald only. No exceptions.
3. **H1 and H2 are always uppercase.** H3 is uppercase. H4 is title case.
4. **Body text color:** Cool White (#F0F2F5) on dark backgrounds. True Black (#111111) on light backgrounds.
5. **Secondary text:** Steel Silver (#8C9EAF) for supporting information, metadata, and captions on dark backgrounds.
6. **Gold text is reserved for emphasis:** CTAs, key data callouts, technical specifications, and the heritage accent. Never for body paragraphs.
7. **Maximum line length:** Body text should not exceed 70 characters per line (approximately 640px at 16px).
8. **Paragraph spacing:** Use 1.5x the body line-height (24px) between paragraphs — 36px margin-bottom.
9. **Letter-spacing:** Captions use 0.12em (wider than standard for industrial label feel). H1 uses tight -0.01em tracking. H3 uses 0.02em.
10. **Heritage font restraint:** Libre Baskerville should appear no more than 1-2 times per page. It is a precision accent, not a workhorse.
11. **No additional typefaces.** The five-font system covers all use cases. Do not introduce new typefaces under any circumstances.

## 5. Imagery

### 5.1 Photography Direction

Photography for this brand is dark, dramatic, and cinematic. Every image should communicate authority, scale, and operational mastery. The visual tone is closer to a feature documentary on industrial engineering than a corporate brochure.

**Composition Ratio:**
- **90% or more** dark, dramatic operational photography — cinematic lighting, industrial environments, equipment at scale, divers at depth.
- **10% or less** atmospheric shots — dark ocean surfaces, industrial textures, equipment details in dramatic lighting.
- **0%** generic stock photography. Zero tolerance. If it looks like it could appear on any diving company's website, it does not belong here.

### 5.2 Photography Style

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Color Grading** | Dark, desaturated, cinematic. Push toward deep navy shadows and electric gold highlights. High contrast. Cool overall temperature with isolated warm gold accents — the glow of a welding arc, the beam of a work light, the last light at twilight. |
| **Lighting** | Dramatic, directional, single-source preferred. Work lights cutting through dark water. Golden arcs from welding operations. Silhouettes against backlit environments. Avoid flat, even lighting entirely. |
| **Subjects** | Equipment in operation. The work, not the worker. Close-up on the weld bead, the instrument reading, the equipment rigged and ready. When divers appear, they are silhouettes or partially visible — helmeted, equipped, anonymous. The operation is the subject. |
| **Setting** | Industrial environments — vessel hulls, port infrastructure, open water, steel decks. The scale should feel enormous. Show the immensity of vessels, the depth of water, the weight of equipment. |
| **Composition** | High contrast. Generous negative space in dark areas for text placement. Favor dramatic angles — low looking up at vessel hulls, wide shots that emphasize scale, tight macro shots that reveal precision. |
| **Mood** | Power. Precision. Weight. Quiet authority. Not adrenaline — controlled intensity. These images should feel like stills from a film about engineering excellence. |

### 5.3 Image Treatment

When applying brand treatment to photographs:

- **Color Overlay:** A Deep Navy (#0B1426) to transparent gradient overlay at 50-70% opacity. Used to unify images with the brand palette and ensure text legibility.
- **Duotone:** For stylized applications, a Deep Navy (#0B1426) and Electric Gold (#D4A017) duotone treatment creates high-impact visuals.
- **Dark Vignette:** Heavy vignetting (darkening edges) reinforces the dramatic, focused aesthetic. Apply generously.
- **Opacity:** When used as a background behind text, apply a dark overlay at 70-85% opacity to ensure text legibility. Lighter overlays are insufficient for this brand's white-on-dark typography.
- **Never apply warm, bright, or saturated color grading.** The palette is cool, dark, and controlled.

### 5.4 Iconography

- Use sharp, angular, geometric line icons with a consistent 1.5px stroke weight.
- Icon color: Cool White (#F0F2F5) on dark backgrounds. Deep Navy (#0B1426) on light backgrounds.
- Optional gold accent: A single Electric Gold (#D4A017) detail per icon — a dot, a line segment, a precision mark.
- Icon corners: Sharp, not rounded. Mitered or beveled line-joins, not round caps.
- Avoid: Rounded, friendly, or organic icon styles. No filled icons with soft edges.
- Grid: Icons should be built on a 24x24px grid with 2px padding.

### 5.5 Graphic Elements

The visual system incorporates industrial and engineering-inspired graphic elements:

- **Technical grid overlays:** Subtle grid patterns suggesting engineering drawings or coordinate systems.
- **Precision measurement marks:** Thin cross-hair or calibration marks at key points in layouts.
- **Angular divider lines:** Sharp diagonal or chevron-shaped section dividers instead of horizontal rules.
- **Coordinate notation:** Small, technical-styled labels (JetBrains Mono, 10px, Steel Silver) positioned at edges of images or sections, referencing depth, position, or specification data.
- **Gold accent lines:** Thin (1-2px) Electric Gold lines used as precision marks, underscores beneath headings, or to delineate key sections.

### 5.6 Imagery to Avoid

- Bright, tropical, clear-water diving scenes. This is not a resort brand.
- Warm, soft, or friendly imagery. No golden-hour lifestyle photography.
- Generic stock photos of any kind — divers, oceans, corporate settings, handshakes.
- Overly polished or retouched imagery that feels artificial.
- Soft lighting, diffused light, or flat exposure.
- Images showing faces prominently. The work is the subject.
- Underwater photos that look like nature documentaries or marine biology content.
- Colorful coral, marine life, or anything that suggests recreational diving.
- Images with visible smiles, casual body language, or relaxed settings.

## 6. Voice & Tone

### 6.1 Voice Characteristics

The Oceantech "Industrial Precision" voice is consistent, deliberate, and weighted. Every word is selected with the same care applied to every weld. Filler does not survive review.

| Characteristic | Description | Example |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **Bold** | Direct, declarative statements. No hedging. No qualifiers. | "Precision is not a goal. It is a requirement." |
| **Precise** | Technical accuracy when specificity demands it. Zero filler. | "AWS D3.6M certified wet welding. ADCI-compliant surface-supplied diving." |
| **Commanding** | Authority earned through restraint, not volume. | "Four decades in the water. The work speaks." |
| **Spare** | Every word earns its place. Fewer words carry more weight. | "We weld. We cut. We inspect. We do not guess." |
| **Elite** | Premium positioning without apology. Confident. | "The standard for commercial diving in the Visayas." |

### 6.2 Tone Spectrum

The tone adjusts by context while the voice remains constant:

| Context | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Website hero** | Maximum impact, declarative | "Engineered for the deep." |
| **Service descriptions** | Precise, technical, authoritative | "Controlled material removal in confined and submerged environments." |
| **About section** | Confident heritage, matter-of-fact | "Our team has been diving since 1981. That's not a tagline." |
| **Safety communications** | Absolute, non-negotiable | "Every dive follows a safety management plan. Full stop." |
| **Technical proposals** | Structured, forensic, defensible | "Scope-based quotation inclusive of mobilization, execution, and documentation." |
| **Social media** | Sharp, proud, minimal | "Another hull. Another specification met. Lapu-Lapu City." |
| **CTA / Contact** | Direct, no excess | "Define the scope. We'll define the solution." |

### 6.3 Writing Guidelines

**General Rules:**

- Write in active voice. Always. "We execute" not "Operations are executed by our team."
- Use short, declarative sentences. If it needs a semicolon, split it.
- Lead with the fact. Let it carry weight. Do not explain what is self-evident.
- Be specific. "46 years" not "decades." "Lapu-Lapu City" not "the Philippines." "AWS D3.6M" not "industry-certified."
- Avoid contractions in all contexts. This brand speaks formally. "We do not" not "We don't."
- Sentence fragments are permitted when they carry impact. "Zero tolerance." "Full stop." "No exceptions."

**Sentence Structure:**

- Prefer: Subject-verb-object. Short. Direct. Heavy.
- Avoid: Passive constructions, nested clauses, hedging language ("we aim to," "we strive for," "we believe").
- Paragraph length: 2-3 sentences for web content. Maximum 4 for any context.
- Let whitespace speak. Silence communicates authority.

**Formatting Principles:**

- Headlines are always capitalized (all-caps for H1/H2/H3).
- Use periods at the end of standalone statements, even in headlines, when the tone demands finality.
- Numbers are written as numerals, not words. "46 years" not "forty-six years."
- Use the em dash ( — ) for emphasis breaks. Sparingly.

### 6.4 Word Choice

**Words to Use:**

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Precision | The defining quality — exactness in everything |
| Engineered | Intentional, designed, not improvised |
| Forged | Shaped through pressure and experience |
| Proven | Backed by record, not promise |
| Deployed | Military/industrial precision — not "sent" or "dispatched" |
| Execute | Decisive action, operational language |
| Zero-compromise | Absolute standard, no negotiation |
| Industrial-grade | Quality tier signaling — above consumer, above standard |
| Specification | Exactness, documented requirements |
| Mastery | Earned excellence through time and discipline |

**Words to Avoid:**

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Friendly | Contradicts the commanding personality |
| Fun | Off-brand entirely — this is serious work |
| Passionate | Vague emotional appeal; show, do not tell |
| Synergy | Corporate emptiness |
| Leverage | Impersonal business jargon |
| World-class | Unsubstantiated superlative; everyone claims it |
| Utilize | Just say "use" |
| Innovative | Overused and meaningless in this context |
| Solutions | Generic; be specific about what we deliver |
| Cutting-edge | Tech-industry jargon; does not fit industrial identity |
| Strive / Aim | We do not aspire. We execute. |
| Stakeholders | Say "clients" or "operators" |

## 7. Dark Mode First

### 7.1 Design Philosophy

This brand is engineered dark-mode-first. Deep Navy and True Black backgrounds are the default, standard treatment for all digital applications. Light mode is a secondary context used only when required by medium (printed documents, certain email clients, regulatory submissions).

This is not an aesthetic choice. It is a brand decision. The dark palette communicates authority, depth, and premium positioning. It sets this brand apart from every competitor operating on a white background with teal text.

### 7.2 Dark Mode Specifications

| Element | Color | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Page background** | `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | Primary surface |
| **Section background (alt)** | `#111111` (True Black) | Alternating sections for depth |
| **Card / Surface** | `#0B1426` at 100% or `#111111` with subtle border | 1px border in `#1A2A3A` for card edges |
| **Primary text** | `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | All headings and body text |
| **Secondary text** | `#8C9EAF` (Steel Silver) | Captions, metadata, supporting text |
| **Accent text** | `#D4A017` (Electric Gold) | CTAs, data callouts, links |
| **Dividers** | `#1A2A3A` | Subtle, 1px, barely visible |
| **Interactive hover** | Gold at 85% opacity | Buttons, links, clickable elements |

### 7.3 Light Mode Specifications (Secondary)

Used for printed documents, PDF reports, light-required email contexts, and regulatory submissions.

| Element | Color | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Page background** | `#F0F2F5` (Cool White) | Clean, clinical |
| **Card / Surface** | `#FFFFFF` (White) | Document surfaces |
| **Primary text** | `#111111` (True Black) | All body text |
| **Headings** | `#0B1426` (Deep Navy) | H1-H4 |
| **Secondary text** | `#8C9EAF` (Steel Silver) | Captions, metadata |
| **Accent** | `#D4A017` (Electric Gold) | Sparingly — key callouts, rules |
| **Dividers** | `#D0D5DC` | Light gray, subtle |

### 7.4 Transition Rules

- Never mix dark and light surface colors within the same section.
- Dark mode sections can use both Deep Navy and True Black to create layered depth — but never Cool White surfaces within a dark layout.
- In light mode, Deep Navy can be used for header bars, footers, and accent sections — but the primary reading area should remain Cool White / White.
- Gold accent usage should increase slightly in dark mode (it reads more naturally against dark surfaces) and decrease in light mode (it can feel garish on white at high saturation).
