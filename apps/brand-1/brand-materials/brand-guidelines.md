## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Story

Oceantech Commercial Diving was established in 2016, but our roots reach back to 1981 — when our founders began diving from a PADI dive shop at Costabella Tropical Beach Resort in Maribago, Mactan Island. They went on to become pioneer PADI instructors in the Philippines, pioneering recreational diving in the Cebu region and training a generation of divers who followed.

Over the decades that followed, they built a depth of knowledge that can only come from time spent beneath the surface: reading currents, understanding marine structures, and developing an instinct for the ocean that no classroom can teach. As a family business, that knowledge has been passed down and refined across generations.

When we formed Oceantech, the purpose was clear — take everything earned across those 45 years and build a commercial diving operation that does things the right way. Based in Maribago, Lapu-Lapu City, in the heart of the Central Visayas, we serve ship owners, port authorities, marine contractors, and energy operators across the Philippines and the wider region.

We are not the newest company in this industry. We are among the most seasoned. Our heritage is our advantage — and it shows in every dive, every weld, every report we deliver.

### Mission

To deliver safe, precise, and dependable commercial diving services built on 45 years of earned expertise — serving the maritime and industrial sectors of the Philippines with the professionalism of an international operator and the responsiveness of a local partner.

### Vision

To be the most trusted name in commercial diving across the Visayas and the Philippines — recognized for our safety record, the quality of our work, and the depth of experience behind every operation.

### Core Values

1. **Safety Without Compromise** — Every dive plan, every procedure, every decision starts with safety. No exceptions, no shortcuts. This is not a policy — it is who we are.

2. **Earned Expertise** — Our knowledge was not learned in a classroom alone. It was built across 45 years in the water — from recreational reefs to commercial hulls. Our founders were pioneer PADI instructors in the Philippines, and that pioneering spirit still drives us today. We know the ocean because we have lived in it.

3. **Honest Work** — Transparent pricing. Honest assessments. We tell you what needs to be done, not what makes us more money. Our reputation is worth more than any single contract.

4. **Local Roots, Professional Standards** — Born in Cebu, built to international standards. We bring global-grade capability without the global-grade overhead.

5. **Respect for the Water** — We started as divers who fell in love with the ocean. That respect carries into every commercial operation. We protect the marine environment while serving the maritime industry.

### Positioning Statement

For ship owners, port authorities, and marine contractors in the Visayas and greater Philippines, **Oceantech Commercial Diving** is the commercial diving company that combines **45 years of diving heritage with modern industrial capability**, because our team has been in the water since 1981 — longer than any local competitor — giving us an unmatched depth of experience that no newly formed crew can replicate.

### Brand Personality

**Archetype:** Sage-Hero Hybrid

- **Sage:** Deep knowledge, earned wisdom, trusted authority — "We've seen it all beneath the surface."
- **Hero:** Courage, action, reliability — "When the job needs doing, we're already in the water."

**Personality Traits:**

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Seasoned | Experienced, weathered, deeply knowledgeable |
| Dependable | Shows up, delivers, every time |
| Warm | Approachable, human, not corporate |
| Precise | Technically excellent, detail-oriented |
| Grounded | Humble confidence, no arrogance |

## 2. Logo

### 2.1 Logo System Overview

The Oceantech logo system consists of four primary components that can be used independently or in combination:

| Component | Description | Primary Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Icon Mark** | Abstract geometric form suggesting depth and precision | Favicons, avatars, app icons, compact spaces |
| **Wordmark** | "OCEANTECH" in bold geometric sans-serif | Headers, signage, wide-format applications |
| **Combination Mark (Horizontal)** | Icon + wordmark side by side | Primary use on website, documents, wide spaces |
| **Combination Mark (Stacked)** | Icon above wordmark | Square formats, social media, vertical spaces |

**Logo Concepts Developed:**
- Abstract geometric "O" icon (depth and descent motif)
- OT monogram (interlocking letterform)
- Hexagonal depth motif (concentric geometric shapes)
- Industrial precision mark (sharp angles with clean curves)
- Heritage modern mark (established feel with contemporary execution)

### 2.2 Approved Color Variations

The logo must only appear in the following approved color treatments:

| Variation | Usage | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Full Color** | Default use on light backgrounds | Deep Ocean Teal icon + Charcoal or Teal wordmark |
| **Single Color — Teal** | Monochrome applications | Entire mark in #0A4D68 |
| **Single Color — Charcoal** | Formal documents, fax, stamps | Entire mark in #2D3436 |
| **Single Color — Black** | Newsprint, low-fidelity reproduction | Entire mark in #000000 |
| **Reversed — White** | Use on dark or photographic backgrounds | Entire mark in #FFFFFF |

Never render the logo in unapproved colors, gradients, or effects.

### 2.3 Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo on all sides equal to the height of the "O" in the icon mark. This zone must remain free of text, imagery, borders, or other graphic elements.

```
    ┌──────────────────────────────┐
    │          (clear)             │
    │    ┌──────────────────┐     │
    │    │                  │     │
    │    │   [LOGO MARK]    │     │
    │    │                  │     │
    │    └──────────────────┘     │
    │          (clear)             │
    └──────────────────────────────┘

    ◄─►  Clear space = height of "O" in icon mark
```

For the wordmark-only configuration, the clear space is equal to the cap height of the letter "O" in "OCEANTECH."

### 2.4 Minimum Size

| Format | Minimum Height | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Digital — Combination Mark** | 40px | Full mark with legible wordmark |
| **Digital — Icon Only** | 24px | Favicon and small UI contexts |
| **Print — Combination Mark** | 15mm | Business cards, small print |
| **Print — Icon Only** | 10mm | Embroidery, small merchandise |

Below these sizes, switch to the icon-only mark.

### 2.5 Incorrect Usage

The following treatments are never permitted:

- **Do not** stretch, skew, or distort the logo in any direction.
- **Do not** rotate the logo.
- **Do not** apply drop shadows, bevels, glows, or 3D effects.
- **Do not** place the full-color logo on a busy photographic background without a solid or semi-transparent backing.
- **Do not** change the relative proportions between the icon and wordmark.
- **Do not** rearrange logo components (e.g., placing the icon to the right of the wordmark in the horizontal configuration).
- **Do not** add outlines or strokes to the logo.
- **Do not** use the logo at low resolution where details become illegible.
- **Do not** place the logo in a box or container unless it is an approved lockup.
- **Do not** use unapproved color combinations (e.g., orange icon with teal wordmark, unless specified in a lockup).

### 2.6 Co-Branding

When the Oceantech logo appears alongside a partner or client logo:
- Maintain equal or greater visual prominence for the Oceantech mark.
- Separate logos with a vertical divider line (1px, #B87333 copper) or adequate white space.
- Never merge, overlap, or interlock the Oceantech logo with another brand's mark.

The following combinations meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast requirements:

| Foreground | Background | Contrast Ratio | Use Case |
|------------|------------|----------------|----------|
| `#F8F9FA` (Off-White) | `#0A4D68` (Teal) | 7.2:1 | White text on teal backgrounds |
| `#F8F9FA` (Off-White) | `#2D3436` (Charcoal) | 14.5:1 | White text on dark backgrounds |

**Caution:** Sunset Orange on white does not meet AA for small text. Use it only for large headings, icons, or non-text decorative elements when placed on light backgrounds. For small orange text, use on a dark background.

### 3.6 Color Combinations to Avoid

- Sunset Orange text on Deep Ocean Teal background (insufficient contrast for readability).
- Copper/Bronze text on Off-White background at small sizes (insufficient contrast).
- Any brand color on a photographic background without a solid or semi-transparent overlay.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Typeface System

The Oceantech type system uses four typefaces, each serving a distinct role:

#### Montserrat — Primary Heading Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Geometric sans-serif |
| **Weights Used** | Bold (700), SemiBold (600), Medium (500) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Modern, authoritative, structured |
| **Usage** | All headings (H1–H4), navigation labels, button text, hero headlines |

Montserrat provides the bold, geometric presence that anchors the brand's visual authority. Its clean geometry communicates precision and modernity while remaining approachable.

#### Playfair Display — Heritage / Serif Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Transitional serif |
| **Weights Used** | Regular (400), Italic (400i) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Established, editorial, refined |
| **Usage** | "Est. 1981" heritage callouts, editorial pull quotes, decorative accents, testimonial attributions |

Playfair Display is used sparingly to introduce moments of heritage and gravitas. It should never replace Montserrat for structural headings — it is reserved for accent moments that communicate legacy and craftsmanship.

#### Inter — Body Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Humanist sans-serif |
| **Weights Used** | Regular (400), Medium (500) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts — free, open source |
| **Character** | Clean, highly readable, neutral |
| **Usage** | Body text, paragraphs, descriptions, form fields, list items, general UI text |

Inter is the workhorse of the type system. Optimized for screen readability, it keeps long-form content comfortable and accessible.

#### JetBrains Mono — Technical / Monospace Font

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Category** | Monospace |
| **Weights Used** | Regular (400) |
| **Source** | JetBrains — free, open source |
| **Character** | Technical, precise, data-oriented |
| **Usage** | Technical specifications, code references, data tables, measurement values, certification numbers |

JetBrains Mono is used when displaying technical data, certifications, or specifications where a monospaced font improves scannability and precision.

### 4.5 Typography Rules

1. **Heading color:** H1 and H2 should use Deep Ocean Teal (#0A4D68). H3 and H4 should use Charcoal (#2D3436).
2. **Body text color:** Always Charcoal (#2D3436) on light backgrounds, Off-White (#F8F9FA) on dark backgrounds.
3. **Maximum line length:** Body text should not exceed 75 characters per line (approximately 680px at 16px).
4. **Paragraph spacing:** Use 1.5x the body line-height (24px) between paragraphs — i.e., 36px margin-bottom.
5. **Letter-spacing:** Captions and overline labels use 0.08em letter-spacing. All-caps headings (when used) should use 0.05em letter-spacing to prevent cramping.
6. **Heritage font restraint:** Playfair Display should appear no more than 2–3 times per page. It is an accent, not a workhorse.
7. **No decorative fonts:** Do not introduce additional typefaces. The four-font system covers all use cases.

## 5. Imagery

### 5.1 Photography Direction

Photography is the single most important element in communicating Oceantech's authenticity. Every image should reinforce the brand's core identity: seasoned, dependable, warm, precise, and grounded.

**Composition Ratio:**
- **80% or more** authentic operational photography — real divers, real equipment, real job sites, real Cebu waters.
- **20% or less** environmental/atmospheric shots — ocean textures, port scenery, equipment details, Cebu landscapes.
- **0%** generic stock photography of divers, underwater scenes, or corporate settings.

### 5.2 Photography Style

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Color Grading** | Warm tones. Push toward teal shadows and orange/amber highlights. The overall palette should feel like golden hour near the water — warm but not oversaturated. |
| **Lighting** | Prefer natural light. Soft, warm, directional. Avoid flat, clinical lighting or heavy flash. |
| **Subjects** | Real crew members in action. Candid or semi-candid — not stiffly posed. Show equipment in use, not staged on a table. |
| **Setting** | Cebu waters, local ports, actual vessels. The environment should be recognizable as Southeast Asian maritime — not generic "blue ocean." |
| **Composition** | Give subjects room. Use negative space for text overlays. Favor wide environmental shots for hero sections, tighter crops for team/service features. |
| **Mood** | Confident. Calm competence. Not dramatic or adrenaline-fueled — this is professional work, not adventure tourism. |

### 5.3 Image Treatment

When applying brand treatment to photographs:

- **Overlay:** A subtle teal-to-transparent gradient overlay (from left or bottom) can unify images with the brand palette. Use sparingly.
- **Duotone:** For stylized applications (social media covers, section backgrounds), a teal (#0A4D68) and warm amber duotone treatment is approved.
- **Opacity:** When used as a background behind text, reduce image opacity to 15–25% or apply a dark overlay at 60–70% opacity to ensure text legibility.

### 5.4 Iconography

- Use simple, geometric line icons with a consistent 2px stroke weight.
- Icon color: Deep Ocean Teal (#0A4D68) on light backgrounds, Off-White (#F8F9FA) on dark backgrounds.
- Icon style: Rounded line-caps, minimal detail, single-weight strokes.
- Avoid filled icons or overly detailed illustrations for UI contexts.
- Service icons may incorporate Sunset Orange as a small accent detail (e.g., a dot, a highlight segment).

### 5.5 Imagery to Avoid

- Stock photos of generic divers in crystal-clear tropical water (this is not a resort brand).
- Overly polished corporate imagery with staged handshakes or conference rooms.
- Cold, clinical, or desaturated color tones.
- Underwater photos that feel like nature documentaries rather than work sites.
- Images showing outdated, damaged, or unsafe equipment.
- Imagery from locations that are clearly not the Philippines or Southeast Asia.

## 6. Voice & Tone

### 6.1 Voice Characteristics

The Oceantech voice is consistent across all communications. It reflects who we are — not a marketing persona.

| Characteristic | Description | Example |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **Experienced** | Confident without arrogance. We let our track record speak. | "45 years in the water taught us what matters." |
| **Authentic** | Real language, not corporate-speak. We talk like people who actually do this work. | "We didn't start as a business. We started as divers." |
| **Locally Proud** | Cebu-rooted, regionally capable. We own where we come from. | "From Maribago to every port in the Visayas." |
| **Technically Credible** | Precise when the context demands it. We know the standards. | "AWS D3.6M certified wet welding, ADCI-compliant surface-supplied operations." |
| **Warm** | Approachable and human. We build relationships, not just client lists. | "We're straightforward people, and we're happy to talk through what you need." |
| **Heritage** | Pioneering credibility rooted in family and community. | "Pioneer PADI instructors in the Philippines — a family tradition of diving excellence rooted in Cebu." |

### 6.2 Tone Spectrum

The tone adjusts by context while the voice remains constant:

| Context | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Website hero section** | Bold, confident, warm | "Commercial diving built on 45 years of ocean experience." |
| **Service descriptions** | Clear, precise, professional | "We perform oxy-arc and thermic cutting on submerged steel, concrete, and mixed structures." |
| **About page** | Personal, storytelling, heritage-forward | "Our founders have been in the water since 1981 — first as recreational divers working Cebu's reefs." |
| **Safety communications** | Direct, serious, authoritative | "Every dive follows a safety management plan. No exceptions." |
| **Social media** | Conversational, proud, community-oriented | "Another clean hull, another satisfied captain. Good morning from Lapu-Lapu." |
| **Proposals / Formal docs** | Professional, structured, credible | "Oceantech Commercial Diving proposes the following scope of work..." |
| **Emergency response** | Calm, decisive, reassuring | "Our team is mobilizing. We'll have divers on site within [timeframe]." |

### 6.3 Writing Guidelines

**General Rules:**

- Write in active voice. Say "We inspect hulls" not "Hull inspections are conducted by our team."
- Use short sentences. If a sentence needs a semicolon, it probably needs to be two sentences.
- Lead with the point. Put the most important information first.
- Be specific. "45 years" is better than "decades." "Lapu-Lapu City" is better than "the Philippines."
- Use contractions in informal contexts (website, social media). Avoid them in proposals and formal documents.

**Sentence Structure:**

- Prefer: Subject-verb-object. Direct. Clear.
- Avoid: Passive constructions, nested clauses, run-on sentences.
- Paragraph length: 2–4 sentences for web content. Never more than 5 for any context.

### 6.4 Word Choice

**Words to Use:**

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Depth | Connects to our work and our experience |
| Precision | Communicates technical excellence |
| Heritage | Signals our long history and earned credibility |
| Trusted | Relationship-focused, not transactional |
| Proven | Backed by track record, not promises |
| Seasoned | Experienced in a warm, human way |
| Crafted | Intentional, not mass-produced |
| Reliable | Dependable, consistent, predictable quality |
| Local | Rooted, present, accessible |

**Words to Avoid:**

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Cutting-edge | Overused tech jargon; does not fit our heritage identity |
| Synergy | Corporate-speak; feels hollow |
| Leverage | Impersonal business jargon |
| Disruptive | We are the opposite — we are established and steady |
| World-class | Vague and unsubstantiated; let the work speak |
| Utilize | Just say "use" |
| Best-in-class | Empty superlative; everyone claims it |
| Solutions | Generic; be specific about what we actually deliver |
| Stakeholders | Say "clients," "partners," or "your team" |
| Innovative | Overused and undefined; show, don't tell |

### 7.3 Social Media Profiles

#### Profile Avatar (All Platforms)

- Use the **icon mark only** (not the full combination mark).
- Full-color version on a white or Off-White (#F8F9FA) circular/square background.
- Ensure adequate padding so the icon is not cropped by platform masking (circular crop on most platforms).
- Minimum output size: 400x400px.

#### Cover Image / Banner

| Platform | Dimensions | Layout |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Facebook | 820 x 312 px | Left-aligned: wordmark + tagline. Right: operational photo with teal overlay. |
| LinkedIn | 1128 x 191 px | Centered: full combination mark over a subtle teal gradient or operational photo. Tagline below. |
| YouTube | 2560 x 1440 px (safe area: 1546 x 423 px) | Centered in safe area: combination mark + tagline. Background: operational photo with 60% dark teal overlay. |

**Cover Design Rules:**
- Background: Operational photography with a teal-to-dark gradient overlay (60–70% opacity) to ensure text legibility.
- Primary text: Wordmark or combination mark in white (reversed).
- Tagline: "Decades below. Built to deliver." in Inter Regular or Montserrat Medium, Off-White.
- Heritage line: "Est. 1981" in Playfair Display Italic, Copper (#B87333) — positioned subtly, not competing with the main mark.
- Keep text within the safe zone for each platform to avoid cropping on different devices.

#### Social Media Content Guidelines

- **Tone:** Conversational, proud, community-oriented. Show the work, not just talk about it.
- **Visual consistency:** Apply warm color grading (teal + orange tones) to all photos. Use brand colors in any graphic overlays.
- **Hashtags:** Use a consistent set — #OceantechDiving, #DeepWorkDoneWell, #CebuDiving, #CommercialDiving, #UnderwaterWelding, and relevant industry/location tags.
- **Post types to prioritize:** Job site photos (with client permission), team highlights, equipment and capability showcases, safety moments, Cebu/local content.
- **Post types to avoid:** Generic motivational quotes, aggressive sales posts, content unrelated to diving or maritime work.

### 7.4 Website Guidelines

**Layout Principles:**
- Maximum content width: 1200px, centered.
- Body text column width: 680px maximum (approximately 75 characters per line).
- Section padding: 80px vertical on desktop, 48px on mobile.
- Card border-radius: 8px.
- Button border-radius: 4px.

**Button Styles:**

**Hover States:**
- Primary button: Darken background by 10% (#D15304).
- Secondary button: Fill with Deep Ocean Teal (#0A4D68), text turns white.
- Tertiary: Underline appears.

**Navigation:**
- Desktop: Horizontal top navigation, Montserrat Medium, 14px, uppercase, 0.05em letter-spacing.
- Mobile: Hamburger menu, slide-in from right, Deep Ocean Teal background, white text.
